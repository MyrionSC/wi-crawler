<p><b>Kuro5hin</b> (<b>K5</b>) (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="/ʒ/: 's' in 'pleasure'">ʒ</span><span title="/ən/: 'on' in 'button'">ən</span></span>/</a></span></span><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><small title="English pronunciation respelling"><i>corrosion</i></small></a><sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">[10]</a></sup>) was a collaborative <a href="/wiki/Internet_forum" title="Internet forum">discussion website</a> founded by <a href="/wiki/Rusty_Foster" title="Rusty Foster">Rusty Foster</a> in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Articles were created and submitted by users and submitted to a queue for evaluation. Site members could vote for or against publishing an article and once the article had reached a certain number of votes, it was published to the site or deleted from the queue.<sup id="cite_ref-pcmag_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcmag-12">[12]</a></sup> The site has been described as "a free-for-all of news and opinion written by readers".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The site was founded by <a href="/wiki/Rusty_Foster" title="Rusty Foster">Rusty Foster</a> in December 1999, having been inspired by <a href="/wiki/Slashdot" title="Slashdot">Slashdot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">[10]</a></sup> Kuro5hin's membership once numbered in the tens of thousands,<sup id="cite_ref-pcmag_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcmag-12">[12]</a></sup> but its popularity declined significantly from its peak in the early 2000s.<sup id="cite_ref-k5-stats_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-k5-stats-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 1, 2016, the site was closed down permanently with all content taken offline.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> However, according to Foster, it may return in the form of an archive of static content at a later date.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuro5hin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All content was generated and selected by the users themselves, with the exception of site news written by the administrators. Registered users would submit stories to the <i>submissions queue</i> where other users would vote +1 FP (<i>front page</i>), +1, 0, or -1. If the story reached a predetermined threshold score, it was posted to the front page or to the relevant section, depending on the proportion of "FP votes". If it failed to make the threshold, other factors (such as number of comments, type of comments, and their ratings) could still cause the story to be posted to a section or to the front page. Otherwise, it was dropped.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>One feature of the story queue was <i>edit mode</i>, in which a story was protected from voting for a period of time during which the author could make changes. Comments could still be made on the story to suggest changes before voting began. These comments were distinguished as being <i>editorial</i> or <i>topical</i>.</p>
<p>A further section was known as the <i>diaries</i>. Having no editing or moderation vetting, diaries were essentially <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">weblogs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dotcom_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dotcom-20">[20]</a></sup> and formed the source of most of Kuro5hin's content by volume. However, unlike the edited article sections, they were not widely syndicated. Other users would also comment on these diaries in the same way as stories but with without the editorial or topical stipulation.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuro5hin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rusty_Foster" title="Rusty Foster">Rusty Foster</a> named Kuro5hin&#160;— pronounced <i><a href="/wiki/Rust" title="Rust">corrosion</a></i>&#160;— as a <a href="/wiki/Pun" title="Pun">pun</a> on his first name.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">[10]</a></sup> The site was powered by the Scoop collaborative system, originally written by Rusty himself,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> with the motto "Technology and Culture, from the Trenches".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Outages">Outages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuro5hin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Outages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2000, the site was temporarily closed due to comment spam and denial of service attacks.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_difficulties">Financial difficulties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuro5hin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Financial difficulties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2002, <a href="/wiki/SourceForge,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="SourceForge, Inc.">OSDN</a> ended the advertising affiliate agreement with Kuro5hin.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In June of that year, Foster suggested he might be forced to sell or shut down the site due to lack of funds. He solicited donations to support the site, and some users were critical of a perceived lack of active management and functional improvement.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subscription">Subscription</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kuro5hin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Subscription">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 25, 2004, Foster closed off new user accounts because a photoshopped pornographic image of his wife had been posted.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On July 13, Foster reopened new user accounts and informed the community that he was abandoning the idea of user sponsorship.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary.2C_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable secondary sources. (August 2012)">non-primary source needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This user sponsorship initiative never came to pass.</p>
